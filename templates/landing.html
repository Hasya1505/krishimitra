India's Farming Journey

From ancient river-valley agriculture to AI-driven precision farming.

~2000 BCE

Indus Valley Agriculture

Early irrigation canals and plough farming along the Indus and its tributaries laid the foundation of organized agriculture in the subcontinent.

1960s

The Green Revolution

High-yield seed varieties, chemical fertilizers, and irrigation expansion transformed India from a food-deficit to a food-surplus nation.

1990s–2000s

Liberalization & Diversification

Market reforms encouraged horticulture, dairy, and export-oriented farming alongside traditional staple crops.

Today

Digital & Precision Farming

Satellite data, IoT sensors, and AI-driven advisories like KrishiMitra now help farmers make sharper, data-backed decisions.
